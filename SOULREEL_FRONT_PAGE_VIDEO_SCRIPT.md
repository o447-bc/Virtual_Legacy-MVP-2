# SoulReel — Front Page Hero Video Script

## Production Overview

| Detail | Spec |
|---|---|
| Total length | 75–85 seconds |
| Platform | HeyGen |
| Avatar | Male, late 40s to mid-50s, warm and approachable. Casual button-down or henley — not a suit. Think "the guy at the family barbecue who always has a good story." |
| Avatar name suggestion | Use a real-looking name in HeyGen's library. Avoid overly polished "news anchor" types. |
| Background | Living room or home office with warm lighting. Bookshelves, a framed family photo slightly out of focus in the background. Feels like home, not a studio. |
| Tone | Conversational, reflective, warm. Like talking to a friend over coffee. Never salesy. |
| Music | Soft, warm acoustic guitar or piano underneath — barely audible. Fades in during the emotional hook, fades out during product demo, returns for the close. Think "indie documentary," not "corporate explainer." |
| Subtitles | Mandatory. White text with dark semi-transparent background. Large enough to read on mobile. |
| Aspect ratio | 16:9 (desktop hero) with a 9:16 vertical cut for mobile/social ads later |

## Scene-by-Scene Script

---

### SCENE 1 — The Emotional Hook
**Time: 0:00 – 0:10**

**Visual:** Avatar on screen, looking directly at camera. Warm lighting. Slight pause before speaking — let the viewer settle in.

**Avatar speaks:**

> "Quick question. What do you actually know about your parents' lives… before you were born?"

**Direction:** Pause for 1.5 seconds after the question. Let it land. The avatar's expression should shift slightly — a small, knowing look, like he already knows the answer is "not enough."

**On-screen text overlay (appears after the pause):**
`What stories are you missing?`
— small, centered below the avatar, in a handwritten-style font. Fades in softly, holds for 2 seconds.

**Why this works:** This question is almost universally disarming. Most people realize immediately that the answer is "not much." It creates an instant emotional gap — the exact gap SoulReel fills.

---

### SCENE 2 — Name the Problem
**Time: 0:10 – 0:25**

**Visual:** Avatar continues speaking. At 0:16, transition to a slow, cinematic B-roll montage overlaid in a picture-in-picture or full-screen swap:
- A family photo album being flipped through (stock footage — warm tones)
- An older person laughing at a dinner table (stock footage)
- A child looking at an old photograph (stock footage)

Avatar voice continues as voiceover during B-roll.

**Avatar speaks:**

> "We've got home videos of birthdays and holidays. But the real stuff — why your mom chose the path she did, what your grandfather learned from his biggest failure, the quiet moments that actually shaped who they are — that just lives in their head."

**Beat. Avatar returns to screen.**

> "And one day… it won't."

**Direction:** The last line is delivered slower, quieter. Not dramatic — just honest. A small exhale after.

**On-screen text overlay (on the last line):**
`These stories disappear. Every day.`
— fades in as he says "it won't," holds for 2 seconds.

**Why this works:** This mirrors the founder story copy almost word-for-word because that copy is already emotionally validated. The B-roll makes it feel universal, not abstract.

---

### SCENE 3 — Introduce SoulReel (The Relief)
**Time: 0:25 – 0:38**

**Visual:** Avatar back on screen, energy shifts slightly — warmer, more forward-leaning. A subtle smile.

**Avatar speaks:**

> "That's why I built SoulReel. It's simple. You pick a question — something like…"

**Visual transition:** Screen splits or transitions to a **screenshot of the SoulReel app** showing the sample question cards. Highlight/zoom on three specific questions:

**Screenshot 1 — Life Story card:**
`"What's the bravest thing you've ever done?"`

**Screenshot 2 — Life Events card:**
`"Tell me about the day you became a parent."`

**Screenshot 3 — Values card:**
`"What value do you most want to pass on to the next generation?"`

Each screenshot holds for ~2 seconds with a subtle zoom-in animation. The question text should be large and readable.

**Avatar voiceover continues during screenshots:**

> "…'What's the bravest thing you've ever done?' or 'Tell me about the day you became a parent.' Questions designed to unlock the stories that actually matter."

**Direction:** The screenshots should look like the real app. Use the actual SoulReel UI — the `SampleQuestionCard` components with the category labels ("Life Story," "Life Events," "Values & Emotions") visible. This builds product credibility.

**Why this works:** Showing real questions makes the product tangible immediately. The viewer starts imagining their own parent answering these — that's the conversion moment.

---

### SCENE 4 — Show How Easy It Is
**Time: 0:38 – 0:52**

**Visual:** Transition to a **screen recording or animated mockup** of the SoulReel recording experience. Show:

1. **(2 sec)** The dashboard with the three content path cards — "Life Story Reflections," "Life Events," "Values & Emotions Deep Dive" — with progress indicators visible
2. **(2 sec)** A question appearing on screen with a large "Record" button
3. **(3 sec)** The recording interface active — camera on, a question displayed, and then an AI follow-up question appearing (e.g., "What made that moment feel so important?")
4. **(2 sec)** The conversation flow — showing the back-and-forth between the user's response and the AI's follow-up

**Avatar voiceover during screen recording:**

> "You just press record and talk. No typing. No editing. Our AI listens to what you say and asks follow-up questions — like a conversation with a good friend who genuinely wants to hear more."

**On-screen text callouts during the screen recording (appear as floating labels):**

- Next to the record button: `No typing required`
- Next to the AI follow-up: `AI asks thoughtful follow-ups`
- At the bottom: `Works on computer, phone, or tablet`

**Direction:** The screen recording should feel real but polished. If you can't do a live screen recording, create a clean animated mockup that matches the actual UI colors (legacy-purple, legacy-navy, the light purple backgrounds). The key moment is when the AI follow-up question appears — that's the "oh, that's cool" moment.

**Why this works:** The biggest objection for this audience is "that sounds hard / I'm not tech-savvy." Showing the actual UI with "just press record" demolishes that objection in 5 seconds.

---

### SCENE 5 — The Sharing / Legacy Angle
**Time: 0:52 – 1:05**

**Visual:** Avatar returns to screen. Tone shifts to something more meaningful — this is the emotional payoff.

**Avatar speaks:**

> "Then you choose who gets your stories. Share them with your kids now. Set a date in the future. Or create a time capsule that opens when the time is right."

**Visual transition at "time capsule":** Show a **mockup or screenshot** of the benefactor/sharing interface:
- A list of benefactors (names blurred or using placeholder names like "Sarah," "Michael," "The Grandkids")
- Access condition options visible: "Share immediately," "Future date," "Time capsule"

Hold for 3 seconds.

**Avatar voiceover continues:**

> "Your stories. Your rules. Your family gets to hear your voice — your real voice — for generations."

**On-screen text overlay:**
`Your stories. Your rules.`
— centered, bold, holds for 2 seconds as the avatar says it.

**Direction:** The word "generations" should land with weight. Not dramatic — just true. The avatar should look like he means it.

**Why this works:** The "time capsule" concept is the emotional differentiator. It transforms SoulReel from "video diary app" to "legacy platform." This is what makes people think "I need to do this for my dad."

---

### SCENE 6 — The Two-Path CTA
**Time: 1:05 – 1:15**

**Visual:** Avatar on screen, slightly more energized. Friendly, inviting.

**Avatar speaks:**

> "You can start preserving your own legacy today — it's completely free, no credit card needed."

**Visual:** As he says this, the **SoulReel hero section** fades in behind him or appears as a side panel, showing the two CTA buttons:
- **"Start Free"** (primary purple button)
- **"Start Their Legacy"** (outline button)

**Avatar continues:**

> "Or — if there's someone in your life whose stories you don't want to lose — you can set it up for them in about two minutes."

**Direction:** On "someone in your life whose stories you don't want to lose," the avatar should slow down slightly and make eye contact with the camera. This is the line that converts the "gift for a parent" persona — likely your highest-value customer.

**On-screen text callouts (appear next to the CTA buttons):**
- Next to "Start Free": `Free forever. No credit card.`
- Next to "Start Their Legacy": `Set it up for a parent or grandparent`

**Why this works:** Explicitly naming both personas removes the "is this for me?" friction. The "two minutes" specificity makes the "set it up for someone else" path feel effortless.

---

### SCENE 7 — Emotional Close
**Time: 1:15 – 1:25**

**Visual:** Avatar on screen. B-roll fades in softly behind — the same warm family footage from Scene 2, but now it feels hopeful rather than melancholy. Music swells slightly (still subtle).

**Avatar speaks (slower, quieter):**

> "Because the stories that matter most… are usually the ones no one thinks to record. Until it's too late."

**1-second pause.**

> "Don't wait for someday."

**Visual:** Screen fades to the SoulReel logo centered on a clean white or light purple background. Below the logo:

`www.soulreel.net`

`Start Free Today`

Hold for 3 seconds.

**Final on-screen elements:**
- SoulReel logo (centered)
- `www.soulreel.net` (below logo)
- `Start Free Today →` (styled as a button, matching the legacy-purple brand color)
- Trust badges in a small row at the bottom: 🔒 `Encrypted` · 🛡️ `Your data, your control` · 👁️‍🗨️ `Never shared`

**Direction:** The closing line is the most important moment in the video. It should feel like a quiet truth, not a sales pitch. The avatar's expression should be genuine — a slight nod, like he's speaking from experience (because the founder literally is).

## HeyGen-Specific Production Notes

1. **Avatar selection:** Choose from HeyGen's "Realistic" tier, not "Stylized." The product is deeply personal — cartoon or overly polished avatars will feel wrong. If HeyGen offers custom avatar creation from a real video, consider having the actual founder record a 30-second calibration clip and use that as the avatar. A real founder face converts significantly better for this type of product.

2. **Scene transitions:** Use simple crossfades between avatar and screenshot scenes. No flashy transitions — they undermine the emotional tone. HeyGen supports scene-based editing; create each scene above as a separate HeyGen scene.

3. **Screenshot overlays:** HeyGen allows you to upload images and position them on screen. For Scenes 3–6, upload the app screenshots and use HeyGen's layout tools to position them as side panels or full-screen backgrounds with the avatar in a smaller window.

4. **Pacing:** HeyGen lets you control speaking speed. Set it slightly slower than default — maybe 0.9x. This content needs room to breathe. Add 0.5–1 second pauses where indicated in the script with "[pause]" or "beat."

5. **Voice:** If HeyGen offers voice cloning, use a warm male voice with slight gravel — think "NPR host," not "movie trailer." Avoid anything that sounds like a text-to-speech default.

6. **Music:** Add music in post-production (or use HeyGen's background music feature if available). Keep it at ~15% volume — it should be felt, not heard. Acoustic guitar or soft piano. No drums, no synths.

## A/B Testing Recommendations

Once the video is live, consider testing these variants:

| Variant | What changes | Why test it |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook A vs Hook B** | A: "What do you know about your parents' lives before you were born?" vs B: "If your dad couldn't tell you his stories tomorrow, how many would you already have?" | Hook B targets the "gift for a parent" persona more directly — may convert better for that segment |
| **With founder story vs without** | Include a 5-second "I built this after my dad's 80th birthday" line in Scene 3 vs skip it | Founder stories build trust but add length — test whether it moves the needle |
| **60-second cut vs 80-second cut** | Remove Scene 5 (sharing/time capsule) entirely | Shorter may perform better on mobile; the sharing feature might be a "discover after signup" moment |
| **Avatar vs real founder** | If the founder can record even a rough version to camera, test it against the HeyGen avatar | Real faces almost always outperform AI avatars for emotional products — but production quality matters |

## Key Conversion Psychology at Work

- **Scene 1** creates an information gap (Loewenstein's curiosity gap theory)
- **Scene 2** activates loss aversion — the fear of losing something irreplaceable
- **Scene 3** provides immediate relief and makes the solution tangible with real questions
- **Scene 4** demolishes the "too hard / too technical" objection with visual proof
- **Scene 5** elevates the product from "tool" to "legacy" — emotional reframing
- **Scene 6** removes friction with two clear paths and "free / no credit card"
- **Scene 7** returns to emotion and creates urgency without being pushy

The video never mentions pricing, features lists, or technical details. It sells the feeling of preserving something irreplaceable — and shows just enough of the product to prove it's real and easy.
