### What this tells us

The market has a clear price cluster at **$99/year** for DIY memoir platforms (StoryWorth, Remento, StoriedLife). These are the products most consumers will compare SoulReel against.

But none of them do what SoulReel does. StoryWorth is text-only. Remento does voice but the AI only transcribes — it doesn't interview. StoriedLife has AI conversations but no video, no benefactor management, no access conditions, no psych tests. Eternos has AI avatars but costs $300–$1,600/year and requires 10+ hours of setup.

SoulReel sits in a gap: **more technology than the $99 products, more accessible than the $300+ products.**

## CRITIQUE: WHAT THE NUMBERS DON'T TELL YOU

### 1. Conversion rate impact is non-linear

Going from $90 to $150 doesn't reduce conversion by a proportional 67%. Pricing research consistently shows that in the $50–$200 range for consumer subscriptions, the conversion impact of a $50 price increase is roughly 15–25% fewer conversions — not 50%. The users you lose are the least committed ones who would have churned anyway.

| Price | Est. Conversion Rate | Paid Users (from 3,333 signups) | Annual Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| $89.99/year | 3.0% | 100 | $8,999 |
| $149/year | 2.5% | 83 | $12,367 |
| $199/year | 2.0% | 67 | $13,333 |

**$149/year generates 37% more revenue than $89.99 despite 17% fewer conversions.**
**$199/year generates 48% more revenue than $89.99 despite 33% fewer conversions.**

This is the classic "fewer customers, more revenue" dynamic. It works as long as the product justifies the price.

### 2. The buyer isn't always the user

A significant portion of SoulReel purchases will be gifts — adult children buying it for aging parents, grandchildren buying it for grandparents. Gift buyers are less price-sensitive. They're buying peace of mind and emotional connection. "$199 to preserve Dad's life stories" is an easy yes for a 40-year-old with disposable income. "$89.99 to preserve Dad's life stories" might actually make them wonder if it's good enough.

### 3. The anchor matters more than the number

If the pricing page shows:
- ~~$199/year~~ → **$149/year** (save $50!)

That's more compelling than just "$149/year." The struck-through price creates perceived value. This is why starting higher gives you more room to maneuver.

### 4. Price signals quality for emotional products

People buying legacy preservation aren't optimizing for cost. They're optimizing for "will this actually work? Will my family treasure this?" A $90/year product feels like a subscription they might cancel. A $149/year product feels like an investment they'll protect. A $199/year product feels like a commitment to something that matters.

Research on pricing psychology for emotional/personal products consistently shows that moderate premium pricing (not the cheapest, not the most expensive) generates the highest satisfaction and lowest churn. Users who pay more value the product more and use it more — which for SoulReel means more conversations, more content, more benefactor engagement, and more word-of-mouth.

## RECOMMENDATION: $149/YEAR ($14.99/MONTH)

### Why $149 is the sweet spot

**1. It's the revenue-maximizing price point.**

$149 generates 37% more revenue than $89.99 with only ~17% fewer conversions. That's the best revenue-per-signup ratio of the three options. You need fewer users to hit the same revenue targets, which means less marketing spend, less free-tier subsidy, and less infrastructure to support.

**2. It positions SoulReel correctly in the market.**

At $89.99, SoulReel is "another StoryWorth alternative." At $149, it's "the premium AI legacy platform." The product genuinely is premium — real-time AI interviewing, voice + video, conditional access, psych tests. Pricing it at $89.99 undersells what you've built. Pricing it at $199 oversells it before you have the brand recognition to justify it.

$149 sits exactly in the gap between the $99 DIY tools and the $300+ Eternos. That gap is where SoulReel belongs.

**3. It creates room for strategic discounting.**

Launch at $149. Offer early adopters $99/year as a "founding member" rate. Run holiday promotions at $119. Give the win-back system a $99 re-engagement offer. Every discount feels generous because the anchor is $149.

If you launch at $89.99, you have no room to discount. You can't run a "$69 sale" without devaluing the product. You're stuck.

**4. It filters for committed users.**

Users who pay $149/year are more likely to actually use the product, complete their legacy, set up benefactors, and tell their friends. Users who pay $89.99 are more likely to sign up, do 3 conversations, and forget about it. The higher price creates a self-selection effect that improves engagement, reduces churn, and generates better word-of-mouth.

**5. The monthly option at $14.99 is still accessible.**

$14.99/month is less than Netflix. It's less than a single lunch out. For a user who just completed Level 1 for free and felt the emotional impact of recording their childhood stories, $14.99/month to unlock "Messages to Loved Ones" is not a hard sell.

### Pricing page framing

Don't compare to StoryWorth. Compare to what the product replaces:

> "A professional memoir costs $3,000–$10,000. A ghostwriter takes months.
> SoulReel gives you an AI interviewer that guides you through your entire life story —
> childhood to legacy messages — in your own voice, at your own pace.
> Share it with family on your terms. $149/year."

Then below that, the feature comparison with the free tier. The annual plan should be the default selection with "Most Popular" badge. The monthly option is visible but secondary.

### Launch strategy

1. **Soft launch at $99/year** for the first 100 users ("Founding Member" rate, locked in for life)
2. **Raise to $149/year** once you have testimonials and social proof
3. **Keep $99 as a promotional price** for campaigns, partnerships, and win-back emails
4. **Never go below $99** — that's your floor, and it still has 91% margin

This gives you the best of both worlds: early traction at a competitive price, then premium positioning as the product matures.
