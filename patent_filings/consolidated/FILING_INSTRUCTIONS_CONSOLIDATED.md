## Step 1 — Confirm Micro Entity Eligibility

Before filing, you must confirm you qualify for micro entity status. This gives you an 80% discount on USPTO fees ($65 per provisional instead of $325).

You qualify under the gross income basis (Form PTO/SB/15A) if ALL of the following are true:

- [ ] You qualify as a small entity: you are an individual who has not assigned, licensed, or conveyed an ownership interest in the invention to any entity with 500 or more employees
- [ ] You have not been named as an inventor on more than 4 previously filed U.S. nonprovisional patent applications (provisional applications do NOT count toward this limit)
- [ ] Your gross income in the preceding calendar year (2025) did not exceed **$251,190** (this is the current limit as of September 2025, based on 3× median U.S. household income)
- [ ] You have not assigned, granted, or conveyed an ownership interest to any entity whose gross income exceeded $251,190

If any of these are not true, you must file as a small entity ($130 per application) or large entity ($325 per application) instead.

**Important**: You must re-verify eligibility each time you pay a fee at the micro entity rate. If your income or filing count changes, you must notify the USPTO.

## Step 2 — Prepare the PDF Documents

Convert each .md filing to PDF format. The USPTO accepts PDF uploads via Patent Center.

### Formatting Requirements

- **Page size**: US Letter (8.5" × 11")
- **Margins**: At least 1 inch on all sides (top margin at least 0.75")
- **Font**: Times New Roman, 12 point (or equivalent serif font)
- **Line spacing**: 1.5 or double-spaced (double-spaced preferred)
- **Page numbers**: Sequential, centered at bottom of each page
- **Paragraph numbers**: Already formatted as [0001], [0002], etc. in the documents — keep these as-is
- **Drawings**: The ASCII diagrams in the filings are acceptable for provisional applications. For the non-provisional filing (within 12 months), you will need formal patent drawings prepared by a technical illustrator.

### Recommended Conversion Tools (macOS)

Any of these will work for .md to PDF:

- **Pandoc** (command line): `pandoc Filing_A_Platform.md -o Filing_A_Platform.pdf --pdf-engine=xelatex -V geometry:margin=1in -V fontsize=12pt -V mainfont="Times New Roman" -V linestretch=1.5`
- **Marked 2** (macOS app): Open .md file, File → Export as PDF, set margins and font in preferences
- **VS Code**: Install "Markdown PDF" extension, right-click → "Markdown PDF: Export (pdf)"
- **Online**: Use a service like markdowntopdf.com (review privacy policy before uploading patent content)

### What Each PDF Should Contain

Each PDF is a single document containing all sections in order:
1. Title of the Invention
2. Inventor(s)
3. Cross-Reference to Related Applications
4. Field of the Invention
5. Background of the Invention
6. Summary of the Invention
7. Detailed Description of the Invention
8. Brief Description of the Drawings
9. Drawings (the ASCII/text diagrams)
10. Claims (optional for provisionals but included for scope)
11. Abstract

Do NOT include the "PROVISIONAL PATENT APPLICATION" and "UNITED STATES PATENT AND TRADEMARK OFFICE" header lines in the PDF — those are just labels in the .md files. The cover sheet (PTO/SB/16) serves as the official header.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need a patent attorney to file a provisional?**
A: No. Provisionals are not examined and do not require formal claims. You can file pro se (on your own). However, for the non-provisional filing, a patent attorney is strongly recommended.

**Q: Can I make changes to the provisional after filing?**
A: No. Amendments are not permitted in provisional applications after filing. If you discover something you want to add, you can file a new provisional with the additional material.

**Q: What if I want to add the avatar features to Filing A later?**
A: You cannot amend Filing A. But Filing B already covers the avatar features. When you file non-provisionals, each non-provisional can claim priority to whichever provisional contains the relevant description.

**Q: What does "Patent Pending" mean?**
A: It means you have a pending patent application. It does not grant any enforceable rights. It serves as notice to potential infringers and establishes your priority date.

**Q: What if someone files a similar patent after my provisional but before my non-provisional?**
A: Your provisional filing date is your priority date. As long as your non-provisional is filed within 12 months and claims benefit of the provisional, your earlier filing date prevails.

**Q: Can I show the provisional to investors or partners?**
A: Yes. Provisionals are not published by the USPTO (they remain confidential unless a non-provisional is filed and published). You can share the content under NDA. The "Patent Pending" status may increase investor confidence.
